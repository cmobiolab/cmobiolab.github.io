Research Themes

Our group investigates molecular systems where light drives biological and chemical processes, bridging quantum mechanics and statistical thermodynamics.

🌿
Photosynthesis
Excitonic energy transfer and vibronic coupling in light-harvesting complexes. Absorption and fluorescence spectra via QM/MM trajectories.
Vibronic spectraExcitonic couplingChlorophylls
💊
Photodynamic Therapy
Electronic structure and photosensitisation mechanisms of porphyrins and graphene quantum dots as PDT agents. Singlet oxygen generation pathways.
PDTPorphyrinsGQDs¹O₂
☀️
Dye-Sensitized Solar Cells
First-principles characterisation of organic dye photosensitisers on TiO₂ surfaces, charge-transfer excited states, and electron injection dynamics.
DSSCTiO₂Charge TransferTDDFT

Biomolecular Simulations
Molecular dynamics of proteins and flavin cofactors in explicit solvent. Force-field parametrisation, RESP charges, free energy calculations.

Methods & Software

Our multiscale strategy spans from classical force fields to correlated wavefunction theory, grounded in rigorous physical chemistry.

01
Quantum Chemistry — DFT, TDDFT, MP2 with PySCF and Gaussian; basis sets up to cc-pVTZ.
02
Molecular Dynamics — Classical MD with AMBER (pmemd.cuda) and OpenMM; AMOEBA polarizable FF.
03
QM/MM — Electrostatic and polarizable embedding; S-QM/MM (Coutinho/Canuto) methodology.
04
Spectroscopy — Vibronic spectra via DREEM; lineshape functions, Huang-Rhys factors, FFT-based integration.
Typical Simulation Workflow
System Setup — topology, RESP charges, solvation box
MD Equilibration — minimization → NVT → NPT
Production MD — ns–μs trajectories (GPU)
QM/MM Snapshots — frame extraction & QM region
TDDFT Calculations — excited states per snapshot
Spectral Analysis — DREEM lineshape & Huang-Rhys
