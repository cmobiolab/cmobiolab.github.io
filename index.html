Computational Multiscale Modelling of Organic & Biomolecules

We develop and apply multiscale protocols that use QM/MM, molecular dynamics, and quantum chemistry to understand light-driven processes in biology and materials science and to study protein structural changes.

Focus Areas

Research Themes

Our interdisciplinary group investigates molecular systems where light drives biological and chemical processes, bridging quantum mechanics and molecular dynamics. We are also interested in protein structure and function studies, using the multiscale approaches.

🌿
Photosynthesis
Excitonic energy transfer and vibronic coupling in light-harvesting complexes. Absorption and fluorescence spectra via QM/MM trajectories.
Vibronic spectraExcitonic couplingChlorophylls
💊
Photodynamic Therapy
Electronic structure and photosensitisation mechanisms of porphyrins and graphene quantum dots as PDT agents. Singlet oxygen generation pathways.
PDTPorphyrinsGQDs¹O₂
☀️
Dye-Sensitized Solar Cells
First-principles characterization of organic dye photosensitizers on 2D materials surfaces, charge-transfer excited states, and electron injection dynamics.
DSSC2D MaterialsCharge TransferTDDFT
🧬
Biomolecular Simulations
Molecular dynamics of proteins and organic ligands in explicit solvent. Force-field parametrization, RESP charges, free energy calculations.
AMBEROpenMMAMOEBAFlavins
🛢️
Naphthenic Acids Investigation
Molecular dynamics studies of NA present in crude oil. The group applies theoretical methods to understand this species' behavior in water and oil, aiming to study its environmental toxicity.
Naphthenic AcidsCrude OilEnvironmental ToxicityMD
⚛️
Polarizable QM/MM
Polarizable embedding (QM/MMpol, DRF/ESPF) for accurate modelling of environmental effects on electronic properties and optical spectra.

Computational Toolkit

Methods & Software

Our multiscale strategy spans from classical force fields to correlated wavefunction theory, grounded in rigorous physical chemistry.

01
Quantum Chemistry — PySCF and Gaussian.
02
Molecular Dynamics — AMBER, GROMACS, and OpenMM; advanced FF.
03
QM/MM — OpenESPF and Gaussian.
04
Spectroscopy — Vibronic spectra via DREEM; lineshape functions, Huang-Rhys factors, FFT-based integration.
